MT. JOY

ABOUT

Mt. Joy is an American indie rock band formed in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and based in Los Angeles, California. The band consists of Matt Quinn (vocals, guitar), Sam Cooper (guitar), Michael Byrnes (bass), Sotiris Eliopoulos (drums), and Jackie Miclau (keyboards). They debuted in 2016 with their single "Astrovan" and have since released three studio albums: "Mt. Joy", "Rearrange Us", and "Orange Blood".

Their music blends alternative rock, indie rock, folk rock, blues rock, and Americana. The band's journey began with high school friends reuniting in Los Angeles, leading to the formation of Mt. Joy. They have charted at the top spot of the Billboard Triple A charts with "Silver Lining" and embarked on headlining tours across North America and Europe, in addition to performing at major festivals.

Mt. Joy is also known for their activism, contributing to causes such as Campaign Zero and serving as ambassadors for the voter registration organization, HeadCount. Their story is one of serendipitous reunions, rapid streaming success, and a commitment to making music that resonates with a broad audience while making a difference.
